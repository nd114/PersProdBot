## Daily anchors (every day)
These don't move regardless of day type:
- Prayer: 2.5 hrs (split however works — e.g. 1hr morning / 1hr midday /
  30min evening; or one long block if that suits you better)
- Bible: your weekly portion (~13 ch/day average, see `bible.md`)
- 30-min do-nothing: genuinely unstructured. Not a buffer for overflow
  work, not scrolling-as-rest — actual nothing. Put it where the day
  tends to get noisiest, as a release valve.
- Sleep: fixed wind-down and wake time, held even on Thu/Sun.

## Work-day structure (Mon, Tue, Wed, Fri, and a shorter version on Sat)

You mentioned splitting work into 2 session blocks — 4:2, 4:3, 3:3, 4:4,
sometimes a single 5hr push. That's a good instinct: named blocks with a
real stop between them beat one long undifferentiated stretch. A shape to
try:

**Block 1 (deep work)** — your least-interruptible hours, on the single
hardest or highest-leverage thing for the day. Write the one-sentence
target *before* you start ("by the end of this block, X exists / is
decided / is sent").

**Reset** — 30–45 min. Not the do-nothing block necessarily; can be food,
a walk, admin triage.

**Block 2** — second priority item, or the delegable/admin-heavy stuff
that doesn't need peak focus.

**Optional Block 3** on 5hr days — same rule: name the target before you
start.

Saturday's block is shorter and ring-fenced to leave room for Sunday's
pastoral prep — don't let Saturday's business block creep into that time.

## On the "days blazing past" problem

You noticed weekdays at home have felt less engaging than weekends the
last two weeks, and some weekdays *have* felt sharp — which suggests it's
not the day of the week, it's something about how those specific days
were set up. Things that reliably change this, worth testing rather than
assuming:

- **A written target before Block 1 starts**, every time, even one line.
  Days without a stated target are the ones most likely to "blur."
  Log this in the daily log and check pattern afterward.
- **A location or context switch** for at least one block a week —
  library, café, coworking space. You don't need it daily, but if
  weekends already feel more engaging partly because they look
  different, borrowing that difference on 1–2 weekdays is worth testing.
- **A hard stop time on Block 2**, announced to yourself in advance.
  Open-ended blocks are where days go soft.
- **End-of-day 2-line note** in the daily log: what got done, what
  didn't. This alone tends to sharpen the next day, because you're not
  vaguely aware you were "busy" — you have a specific record.

Treat this as a hypothesis to test over the first 2 weeks and revisit in
the first monthly review, not a fixed rule yet.

## Delegation
See `business.md` for the per-organization delegate /
don't-delegate list. General principle worth stating once, here: if a
task is delegable but keeps landing on your desk anyway, that's usually a
system gap (no one trained, no clear owner) rather than a you-problem —
worth naming explicitly rather than absorbing.

# Eating Well in the GTA — Sourcing Notes

Reframe first: for the fat-loss and "eating healthier" goals, whole and
minimally-processed food with enough protein does most of the work.
Organic matters less than that — so treat it as a nice-to-have layer, not
a prerequisite, and the "needle in a haystack" feeling should mostly
disappear.

## Lower-effort than hunting store shelves: organic produce box delivery
- **Mama Earth Organics** (Toronto, part of the Fresh City family) —
  weekly subscription produce/pantry boxes, customizable, delivers across
  much of the GTA. Confirmed active as of mid-2026. Lowest-effort option
  if the goal is consistency without weekly shopping trips.
- Similar model: **Fresh City Farms** itself runs the same kind of
  subscription box.

## Dedicated organic/bulk grocers
- **Karma Co-op** (Annex, Toronto) — member-owned, bulk + organic +
  local, open to non-members too. Confirmed open in 2026.
- **The Big Carrot** (Danforth) — long-running organic/natural grocer.
- **Fiesta Farms** (Christie/Dupont) — independent grocer with a strong
  organic and local produce section alongside regular groceries, useful
  if you want one stop rather than a specialty-only trip.

## Farmers' markets (seasonal, best Jun–Oct — lines up with this sprint)
- **St. Lawrence Market** (Saturday farmers' market) — Toronto.
- Local municipal farmers' markets run seasonally across Mississauga,
  Brampton, Markham, Oakville, etc. — worth checking your specific city's
  markets page since exact days/locations shift year to year.

## Practical approach for the 90 days
1. Set up (or confirm) one recurring produce box (Mama Earth / Fresh
   City) as the default baseline — removes the weekly "where do I even
   find this" decision entirely.
2. Use a bulk/organic grocer (Karma, Big Carrot, or Fiesta Farms,
   whichever is closest to your regular routes) for protein, staples, and
   anything the box doesn't cover.
3. Treat regular grocery stores as the fallback, not the failure — most
   now carry a reasonable organic produce section; you don't need to
   drive across the city to stay on-plan.
